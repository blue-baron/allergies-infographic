8 foods account for 90% of all food allergy reactions.

Milk, eggs, peanuts, tree nuts, wheat, soy, fish, and shellfish.

Food Allergy prevalence - percentage of the US population

  • Peanut - 1.3%,
  • Tree nuts - 0.6%,
  • Fish - 0.4%,
  • Crustaceans - 1.2%,
  • Milk and egg - 0.4%,
  • Soy - 0.1%,
  • Wheat - 0.1%.

10% of Australian babies have a food allergy by 12 months.

By adulthood only 1.3% of adults still have a food allergy.

80% of babies with an egg allergy can eat egg baked into cake.

Peanut is the most common allergen affecting 3% of children.

In the next 50 years the number of Australian patients affected by allergic diseases is expected to increase from 4.1 million to 7.7 million.
